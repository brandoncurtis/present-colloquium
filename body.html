Why Study Atmospheric Plasma Devices?

Plasma's potential in medicine

" Chronic wounds, fungal skin and nail infections, and colonization with multidrug-resistant pathogens like (MRSA) often pose significant therapeutic and economical problems ... Our data suggest plasma as a possible new option in the treatment of microbial, fungal, and parasitic dermal infections."

— "Plasma Medicine in Dermatology", Clinical Plasma Medicine, 2012 [1]

[1] DOI:10.1615/PlasmaMed.2014006217       [2] DOI:10.1051/epjap/2011100452

Lack of prior control work on atmospheric pressure plasmas

If an atmospheric pressure plasma source may be useful for biomedical application ... Is the medical effect reliable?   Is the medical effect well-controllable?  Can a quick medical effect be realized?

— "Basic requirements for plasma sources in medicine", EPJ Applied Physics, 2011 [2]

Challenges in Plasma Device Control

"Dosage" is a complex quantity

reactive chemistry, photons, electric fields, heat

→ need to measure and control

Wide range of relevant timescales

  • electrical (microseconds)
  • thermal (seconds to minutes)
  • chemical (minutes to hours)
  • biological (hours to days)

Nonlinear functional relationships in biological dose response and plasma physics

electronic excitation (kHz-MHz) (µs-ms)
gas temperature (degrees per ms)
surface temperature (seconds)
chemical flux (minutes)
biological response (minutes-hours)
realtime control with constrains
nonlinear input-output relationships (positive feedback)
nonlinear dosage response
